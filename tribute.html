Contributions to Computer Science

Charles Babbage studied at Trinity College, Cambridge, where he first had the idea of mechanically calculating mathematical tables. In 1823, he obtained government support to design the Difference Engine, capable of storing data for later processing. He later conceptualized the Analytical Engine, the precursor of modern digital computers, incorporating features such as punched card instructions, a memory unit, and sequential control.

In 1991, British scientists successfully built the Difference Engine No. 2 based on his specifications, proving his ideas were viable. Beyond computing, Babbage contributed to the modern postal system, actuarial tables, the speedometer, and the train cow-catcher.
